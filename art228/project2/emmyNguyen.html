Bánh Bò Nướng

Vietnamese Honeycomb Cake

4/5

jump to recipe by Emmy Nguyen

March 6, 2023

ingredients

  • 12 eggs
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 tbsp butter (melted)
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla
  • 2 cup tapioca starch
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp pandan extract
  • cooking spray

Prep Time: 20mins

Cook Time: 1hr

Servings: 8

Description

Bánh Bò Nướng is a chewy and sweet tapioca sponge cake. The reason it’s called honeycomb cake is because if done correctly, it produces a honeycomb like appearance on the inside. This baked version is my favorite, it produces a nice sweet brown crust on the outside.

This is a recipe passed down from my grandma– it is special because it reminds me of my childhood and Asian heritage. Her biggest piece of advice for this recipe is to take your time and exercise a lot of patience for the best results!

Directions

    preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Prep your pan by spraying it with a cooking spray; my preferred one to use is avocado oil.

    2. combine ingredients

  2. Crack 12 eggs into a bowl and whisk until mixed. Then add in coconut milk, butter and vanilla and continue mixing for 5 mins. (tip: be careful not to over whisk or this will affect the honeycomb texture.)
  3. Add flour and sugar and whisk for 8-10 mins.
  4. Add pandan extract/food color. Whisk until combined.
  5. Add in baking powder, and whisk for 3 more mins.

    6. bake

  6. Pour batter through a strainer right in the middle of the bundt pan. If neccessary, use a spatula to distribute the batter evenly through the strainer and in the pan.
  7. Bake for 55 mins. (important: do not open oven during cooking!)
  8. After it’s done baking, turn off the oven and let it sit in the oven for 5 mins.
  9. After 5 mins, crack open the oven door and leave it open for 5 mins before removing the cake. Serve in slices!
